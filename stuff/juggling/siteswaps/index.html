Vanilla Siteswaps

1 Balls

1

First siteswap to learn! Start with one ball in your R hand, and pass it quick to your L hand. Repeat! Only note: the passages must be very quick and horizontal, almost downwards directly to the other hand.

20

Even easier than 1! Just hold one ball in your R hand and do nothing… This pattern is asymmetric, you can start with your ball in your L hand instead!

300

Useful pattern for your first 2 minutes learning juggling. Take one ball on your R hand and pass it to the L hand with a high pass. The pass should pass in front of your eyes. When it land on your L, throw it again to your R and continue. You see the pattern has 00 after the 3. This means you have time to do something while the ball is in the air. You can try to clap your hands, so to have something like: throw, clap, catch, throw, clap, catch, …

2 Balls

31 – 2 balls shower

The 2 balls shower. While not formally juggling, this is the first pattern everyone can juggle when trying to learn. The only note, learn to make a nice 1 keeping your hands at a good distance and throwing the 1 very horizontally, almost downwards. Will help in the future!

312

Asymmetric version of the two balls shower, or a two balls shower which changes direction continously. The 3 is always the same ball. A kind of very simple ping-pong.

40 – two in one hand

Play two balls in one hand. Practice both L and R. First step to learn four balls!

501

Start with both balls in the same hand and throw a high 5 followed by a quick 1, and repeat. You need to be very quick, not super easy!

3 Balls

3 – 3 balls cascade (Prime!)

The cascade and all its many viariations.

42 (Prime!)

Hold one ball in one hand (the 2), and in the other play the other two in a fountain. You can use this as base for a lot of tricks, like moving the ball doing the 2 back and forth, chasing the other balls or ‘‘playing’’ with them.

423 – half-shower box

One ball hops back and forth (the 3), while the other two hops up and down one in the R and one in the L. The three balls do not mix. Try to make the 4 external.

51 (Prime!)

The 3 balls shower.

441 (Prime!)

A nice pattern. Usefull to improve your 1. See also 4413 and 44133

4413

An asymmetric version of 441. Just add a three at the end.

44133

A slower version of 441. You can extend it with how many 3 you want at the end.

522 (Prime!)

531 (Prime!)

A tricky pattern. Think of it as a 5 followed by a quick 31 swap of the other two balls. Keep the 3 low!

5313

Asymmetric version of 531.

450 (Prime!)

The rhythm of this pattern is strange… “A complete waste of a 5-balls juggler”. You can start with two balls in the L and one in the R; throw L first. To enter while doing 3 just throw a 4: …3334{450}233…

3-balls flash (3 out of 5). Can replace the 00 with a clap or a pirouette.

55050 – 3-snake (Prime!)

3-balls snake (3 out of 5). Take all three balls in one hand and throw them to the other. Throw the last before catching the first. Repeat. Useful to learn 5 balls. (aka 50505)
